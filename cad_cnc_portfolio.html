CAD & CNC Project Portfolio

Selected CAD and CNC fabrication projects that demonstrate design for manufacture, fixturing, and hands-on machining, using tools such as Fusion 360, SolidWorks, and shop-floor CNC equipment.

Incense Stand

Organic form incense holder machined from hardwood.

Designed a modular incense stand inspired by flowing geometry, with focus on clean contours and stable footing. The model was parametrically defined to allow quick adjustment of overall length and slot dimensions.

Skills Demonstrated
  • Fusion 360 three dimensional modeling and parametric design
  • CAM setup for three axis CNC milling
  • Surfacing, sanding, and oil finish workflows

Marble Puzzle

Tight tolerance interlocking wooden puzzle.

Created a multi piece marble puzzle to test repeatability, clearances, and fit on the CNC router. The design required consistent pocket depths and alignment across multiple toolpaths.

Tools used: Fusion 360, Shapeoko router, one eighth inch ball end mill
Material: Maple

Serving Tray

Ergonomic wooden serving tray with CNC sculpted handles.

Designed a tray with integrated handles and rounded pockets for comfortable carrying. The model was set up with separate roughing and finishing passes to balance machining time and surface quality.

Wooden Cutting Boards

Patterned cutting boards using laminated hardwoods.

Developed several cutting board layouts using alternating species and grain directions. The workflow included glue up planning, surfacing toolpaths, edge profiling, and chamfers for hand comfort.

Spoilboard Fixturing Design

Custom spoilboard and workholding layout for repeatable setups.

Designed a spoilboard system with reference fences and threaded inserts to speed up part alignment. The pattern supports both one off projects and small batch production without reclamping reference edges.

Rapid CAD Concept Models

Speed modeled parts for form exploration and design studies.

A collection of concept parts created under time limits to practice fast, clean modeling. These models explore fillets, draft angles, and features appropriate for machining and other fabrication methods.
